    Compliance architecture — read before deploying with real members (Louisiana build)
    
This form runs in de-identified mode: no PHI fields, no transmission, data stays on the device until a user exports it. That design keeps the screening tool itself outside PHI flows and usable by community partners today.

    
The production architecture (identified/PHI use): operate INSIDE HIPAA as a Business Associate of the physician, clinic and FQHC partners — never as a standalone consumer health app. A consumer app outside HIPAA falls under the FTC Health Breach Notification Rule (expanded July 2024), where even an unauthorized disclosure counts as a reportable breach. Inside HIPAA, provider-to-tool data flows ride the treatment/operations pathways under a signed BAA.

    
    
      
  1. BAA with every provider partner (45 CFR 164.504(e)); encryption in transit and at rest, MFA, role-based access, immutable audit logs — built to the 2025 HIPAA Security Rule NPRM standard, which proposes making these mandatory.
    2. 
      
  2. The CBO leg needs layered patient authorization (45 CFR 164.508: who discloses, who receives, what, why, expiration, signature — plus revocation rights). Most community-based organizations are NOT HIPAA-covered, so HIPAA protection ends when data reaches them: use consent-per-referral (the findhelp model) plus a data-use agreement imposing HIPAA-equivalent safeguards on the CBO.
    3. 
      
  3. Sensitive-data segmentation: this form deliberately asks NO substance-use questions. If a deployment adds them, 42 CFR Part 2 applies (enforcement live since February 16, 2026) — separate consent and tagged, limited-visibility handling.
    4. 
      
  4. Louisiana breach law (La. R.S. 51:3071 et seq.): consumer notice within 60 days and Attorney General notice within 10 days of consumer notice, on top of the HIPAA 60-day clock; plus reasonable-security and data-destruction duties.
    5. 
      
  5. Minimum necessary by design: referral partners receive only the flagged domains and program-fit fields — never the full instrument responses unless the receiving clinician needs them.
    6. 
    

    
Why these instruments: the CMS AHC-HRSN core tool (LOINC 96777-8) and Hunger Vital Sign™ are free, validated, public instruments — deliberately chosen over PRAPARE, which requires a NACHC license for any digital/platform build and forbids unapproved question changes. Export fields are structured for Gravity Project / HL7 FHIR SDOH alignment.

    
Why this can also pay for itself (Louisiana): Louisiana Medicaid has reimbursed community health worker services incident-to a supervising physician/APRN/PA since January 1, 2022 (CPT 98960–98962 professional; T1015/H2020 FQHC/RHC; qualifying E/M within 30 days; 2 hrs/day, 10 hrs/month caps; CHW qualified via Louisiana CHW Workforce Coalition-recognized training or 3,000 documented hours). Medicare added CHI codes G0019/G0022 and PIN codes G0023/G0024 in 2024. Screening done by CHWs under clinical supervision is a billable service line — with strict False Claims Act discipline: documented supervision, the initiating visit, time logs, consent and Z-codes (Z55–Z65). Note: G0136 stopped being an SDOH code on 1/1/2026.

    
Framework, not legal advice — engage Louisiana healthcare counsel to paper the BAAs, CBO agreements and consent forms before any PHI deployment.
