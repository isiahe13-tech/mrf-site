    Compliance posture — read before deploying with real members
    
This form runs in de-identified mode: no PHI fields, no transmission, data stays on the device until a user exports it. That design keeps the screening tool itself outside PHI flows.

    
To use it with identified member information (PHI), it must be deployed inside covered infrastructure:

    
    
      
  1. Hosting and storage under a signed Business Associate Agreement (e.g., a covered entity's own systems, or BAA-covered cloud like Google Workspace/Microsoft 365 with BAA).
    2. 
      
  2. Encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest; named-user access controls and audit logging.
    3. 
      
  3. A minimum-necessary data policy, retention schedule, and breach-notification procedure.
    4. 
      
  4. Workforce training for every screener; member consent language approved by the covered entity.
    5. 
    

    
The questions themselves are public, validated instruments: the CMS Accountable Health Communities HRSN Screening Tool core questions and the Hunger Vital Sign™.
