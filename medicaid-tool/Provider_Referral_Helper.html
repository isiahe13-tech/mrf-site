Concept Demo · De-identified by Design · No Patient Data Is Ever Entered Here

Provider Referral & Dual-Trigger Documentation Helper

A clinic-facing order sheet that captures the nutrition-sensitive condition + the screened need + the Z-code in one pass — so referrals arrive clean and medical necessity is documented from day one.

Built to help, never to replace

Your clinical work — the diagnosis, the assessment, the judgment — belongs to you. Nothing on this page changes it, grades it, or tells you how to practice. Several lines are deliberately left open (the RDN assessment content, the condition list, the coder's Z-code preference) because those calls are yours to make, not ours to guess.

The only thing this sheet carries is documentation friction: the screen evidence, the Z-code, the medical-necessity language, and the payable-benefit mapping — the paperwork that gets referrals bounced and gets good programs defunded at renewal.

You win (clean referrals, closed-loop reporting back to you, quality-measure credit). We win (a member who actually gets fed). The member wins most. That's the whole deal.

Step 1 — The clinical trigger (your call, always)

Codes shown are family-level starting points — your coding staff confirms the exact chart code. The tool never overrides the chart.

Step 2 — The screened need (the second trigger)

Positive food screen documents Z59.41 (food insecurity) — or Z59.48 (other specified lack of adequate food) where your coder prefers it. The Z-code is what turns a good deed into countable, renewable evidence.

Step 3 — The funding mechanism (we carry this part)

De-identified by design: patient identifiers are NEVER typed into this tool. The printed sheet has a blank identifier box you complete on paper, inside your clinic's own records environment. The production version lives inside the EHR under a BAA — this concept proves the workflow, not the plumbing.
