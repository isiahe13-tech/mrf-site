Coverage discipline: "3x pipeline" is a relic — required coverage = 1 ÷ win rate, stage-weighted. At sub-20% Medicaid win rates that means 4–7x, built through account DEPTH (multi-threading, MEDDPICC completeness), not volume — there are only ~20 named accounts.

Stage Discipline

  1. Research — map the mechanism, cohort and buying committee (this tool).
  2. Outreach — warm-door first; the hook is on the account's stakeholder map.
  3. Discovery — funding authority, highest-cost cohort, incumbent pain, data path.
  4. Pilot Design — build it in the Pilot Builder; send the one-pager.
  5. Proposal / RFP — the RFP Builder scaffold + compliance matrix.
  6. Won / Parked — won moves to implementation; parked gets a revisit date, never a silent death.
Rule: no account sits without a next action and a date. An empty "next action" is a parked deal you haven't admitted to.

MEDDPICC Per Account — What "Depth" Means on a 20-Account Book

Run every pipeline review on these eight, not on close dates. Work them into each account's notes field.

  • M — Metrics: the plan's own numbers — PMPM trend, readmission penalties, HEDIS SNS-E gap, meal-benefit engagement (often <8%).
  • E — Economic buyer: plan CMO / CFO / VP population health (named on the Stakeholder Map, with source + date).
  • D — Decision criteria: evidence standard, price band, delivery reliability, reporting.
  • D — Decision process: committee, clinical sign-off, pilot-first vs. RFP.
  • P — Paper process: procurement, security review, BAA, contracting — where Medicaid deals actually stall. Map it early.
  • I — Identify pain: the cohort bleeding money (post-discharge CHF, high-acuity complex).
  • C — Champion: who sells when you're not in the room.
  • C — Competition: Mom's Meals, Instacart Health, NourishedRx, regional nonprofits — sell adherence, never price.

    Account Health — The Report That Wins the Renewal

    Renewals die on unprovable value: meal benefits are often used by fewer than 8% of eligible members, and vendors can show shipments but not engagement. This generator turns program data into the renewal story — in the account team's language, per book of business.

    Data honesty: defaults are DEMONSTRATION numbers (say so out loud in any room). The production version runs on real program data; the <8% utilization benchmark is industry/company-reported context, labeled as such in the report.

    Why This Wins Each Book

    MA mode (the retention seat): renewals are decided by the plan's benefit-value review. The two numbers institutional food can't show — activation vs. the <8% norm and the reorder rate (adherence) — are the two numbers this report leads with. The activation plan rides the mid-year unused-benefit notices (first wave mailed June 30–July 31, 2026): the letters that threaten low-utilization benefits become the campaign that fixes them. The competitor evidence bar: Mom's Meals markets its own survey claiming 97% of members who used the meal benefit were likelier to STAY with the plan (vendor-stated — label it) — this report answers a survey claim with program data, and upgrades the story to DOUBLE retention: the benefit retains the member; the report retains the contract.
    Medicaid/SCN mode (the waiver cliff): New York's 1115 authority expires March 31, 2027 — every SCN-funded program's future depends on renewal-proof evidence generated NOW. This pack converts a program's quarter into the evidence a state and CMS conversation needs: enrollment, completion, loop closure, and documented follow-through against the NC HOP $164 PMPM reference point.
    Stars mechanics (the revenue WHY behind MA retention, statutory and stable): plans at 4.0+ stars earn a 5% quality bonus on their benchmark, and the rebate share plans keep rises by rating tier (50% → 65% → 70%). Member-experience measures are scored from real member surveys — which is why a benefit members actually use, love and mention by name is Stars ammunition, and why this report captures satisfaction and reorder behavior every quarter. Weighting precision (2026 Stars): CMS cut member-experience/CAHPS measure weight from 4x to 2x — experience is now ≈22% of the overall rating, down from ≈33% — so activation and adherence evidence leads; satisfaction supports. And the Health Equity Index replaces the reward factor starting with 2027 Stars, making documented reach into high-need members (LIS/dual/disabled) a rated asset — exactly the population a D-SNP meal benefit touches and this report documents. The dollar math is county- and plan-specific — the plan's Stars team owns it; this report feeds it.
    CONCEPT BUILT (v1 skeleton): Provider Referral & Dual-Trigger Documentation Helper — a clinic-facing order sheet capturing the nutrition-sensitive condition + screened need + Z-code in one pass, so referrals arrive clean and medical necessity is documented from day one. Open the working concept. The clinical edges — RDN assessment content, the condition list, coder Z-code preference — are deliberately left open for clinical partners to shape: those calls belong to the clinical team. It also refuses to generate an MTM order when the dual trigger isn't met.

      Field Version for Community Partners & Clinics

      A standalone, mobile-first HRSN field screener ships with this tool — built on the CMS Accountable Health Communities HRSN core questions + the Hunger Vital Sign, sized for a phone, works offline. (HRSN — health-related social needs — is the individual, payable need a plan can act on; the population-level "SDOH" framing stays out of payer rooms.)

      De-identified by design

      No PHI fields; case-reference codes only. Data stays on the device until the screener exports it into the organization's own secure, authorized system.

      Validated instruments

      AHC-HRSN core domains (food, housing, transportation, utilities, safety) with standard scoring, plus meal-program-fit questions that route members to the right service tier.

      Deployment path

      For identified (PHI) use, host inside BAA-covered infrastructure — the form's built-in compliance checklist lists exactly what that requires. A form is only as compliant as the system around it.

      Distribution: send the single HTML file to a partner (email/text/AirDrop) — it opens in any phone browser with no install. Open the field screener
      Modality architecture (evidence-ranked — see Sources): Lead with self-administered digital at a moment of engagement (SMS link, not portal — ~28% of under-$30K households are smartphone-dependent; self-administration yields ~1.3x more honest disclosure of sensitive needs than an interviewer). Backstop with point-of-care self-administration (tablet screening surfaced 35.6% positive vs. 19.6% by portal — the portal misses the neediest members; UPenn JGIM 2024). Reserve CHW/trusted-messenger face-to-face for the positive-dense, digitally-unreachable tail (CMS AHC Model: 1.1M+ screened in person, 37% positive, $200M+ net savings). Phone = warm scheduled follow-up ONLY (~75% never answer unknown numbers; never promise above 20–30% telephonic completion). Realistic contract bands: ~35–55% digital-first, 60–75%+ with the in-person backstop. Report three metrics: completion BY MODALITY, positive rate, and intervention-within-30-days (SNS-E). Passive data (ADI/SVI ≤42% sensitivity; Z-codes on 0.5–2.4% of encounters) targets outreach — it never satisfies the measure.

      What the Questions Are Proving

      1. Medical urgency

      Recent acute use, clinical instability or a serious diet-sensitive condition creates a reason to act now.

      2. Nutrition barrier

      The member cannot reliably obtain, prepare or follow the food plan required for the medical condition.

      3. Intervention fit

      The member can safely receive the service, agrees to participate and is not already receiving a duplicative benefit.
